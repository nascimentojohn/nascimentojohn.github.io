About Me

I'm a dedicated linguist and educator with a Master's degree in Linguistics from the Federal University of Minas Gerais, Brazil. Currently, I'm completing my PhD in Linguistics at the Université du Québec à Montréal, Canada.

My teaching experience spans various prestigious institutions, including McGill University, Bishop's University, and Cégep de Lévis. I specialize in Latin, Ancient Greek, Sanskrit, fundamental linguistics courses, and comparative/historical linguistics.

My research focuses on historical linguistics, specifically comparative Indo-European languages, verbal morphology, grammaticalization, and nanosyntax. I'm passionate about using technological tools to enhance learning outcomes and making linguistics accessible to broader audiences.
