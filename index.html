About Me

I'm a linguist and educator with a Master's degree in Linguistics from the Federal University of Minas Gerais, Brazil. Currently, I'm completing my PhD in Linguistics at the Université du Québec à Montréal, Canada.

My teaching experience includes Latin, Ancient Greek, Sanskrit, fundamental linguistics courses, and comparative/historical linguistics at institutions such as McGill University, Bishop's University, and Cégep de Lévis.

My research focuses on historical linguistics, specifically comparative Indo-European languages, verbal morphology, grammaticalization, and nanosyntax. I use technological tools to improve learning outcomes and work to make linguistics accessible to broader audiences.
